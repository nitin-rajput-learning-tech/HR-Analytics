> **Our position in one line:** A 100% offline, single-file, white-label, deterministic (no-AI) HR-analytics product where PII never leaves the tab — built for India-context SMB/mid-market, consultants, and privacy/air-gap-constrained buyers that every incumbent below structurally prices out or cannot serve.

## 2. Per-Scope Briefs

**Visier People (Threat 2).** Visier is the category-defining dedicated people-analytics platform — a mature, opinionated library of 2,000+ metrics, ~250M benchmarks, ML predictions, and the "Vee" GenAI assistant sitting atop an enterprise HRIS stack. Its moat is data network effects and years of curated analysis depth that no single-tenant tool can replicate. But it is cloud-only with PII ingestion, enterprise-priced (six figures for large deals), and takes 8–16 weeks to deploy. It is overpowered and unaffordable for a single HR team that just wants clean monthly KPIs and a narrative brief — precisely our wedge.

**Workday Analytics (Threat 2).** Workday's stack (People Analytics storyteller engine, Prism ELT, agentic Illuminate, and the 2025 Data Cloud) turns governed system-of-record data into auto-discovered insights, NL narratives, and emailed exec digests — and increasingly into *agentic action* that runs the review cycle. The catch: it is structurally useless without Workday as system of record, costs are opaque (employee-count + modules + consumption Flex Credits), and implementations run 4–18 months. Its AI outputs are probabilistic, raising auditability concerns. We win on "no SoR required," offline privacy, deterministic narratives, and minutes-to-value.

**SAP SuccessFactors / Oracle Fusion HCM (Threat 2).** The two largest HCM suites bolt cloud-only workforce analytics onto their systems of record — thousands of prebuilt KPIs, governed dashboards, vendor benchmarks, and a heavy 2025-26 push into LLM copilots (SAP Joule, Oracle GenAI) for predictive attrition and NL querying. They are economically unreachable below ~1,000 employees (Oracle enforces a hard 1,000-license floor; SAP TCO runs six figures), lock buyers into the underlying suite, and take ~100 days to a year-plus to stand up. Our opening is the India SMB/mid-market they actively price out, sold on offline privacy, INR-native formatting, and reproducible no-AI numbers.

**Microsoft Viva + Power BI (Threat 3 — highest).** This is the most realistic everyday alternative because Microsoft is the default incumbent in any M365/Copilot shop: Viva Glint (credible engagement listening), Viva Insights (passive M365 telemetry, now bundled with Copilot), and Power BI HR templates. But the stack is *fragmented* — no single tool delivers headcount + attrition + diversity + payroll *and* a written narrative; Power BI HR is DIY with no prose output, Insights invites surveillance pushback, and nothing is offline, white-label, or India-tuned. We counter with a unified single-file deliverable, a deterministic CHRO newsletter, and "export-a-clean-dataset-to-Power-BI" as interop rather than head-to-head combat.

**One Model & Crunchr (Threat 2).** Both are dedicated upmarket people-analytics platforms that genuinely solve the hard problem we don't attempt — unifying messy multi-system HR data into one governed model — then layer prebuilt lifecycle metrics, benchmarking, forecasting, and a GenAI NLQ assistant on top (One Model leans data-platform depth; Crunchr leans fast self-service). They are cloud-only, custom-priced (~$30K-$150K+/yr for One Model), and require weeks of implementation. We position as the privacy-safe, instant, deterministic "last mile / pre-platform" tool for buyers their price umbrella excludes.

**ChartHop & Lattice (Threat 2).** These mid-market suites go beyond analytics to *run HR processes* — ChartHop owns org-chart/headcount/comp planning, Lattice owns performance/engagement cycles — creating workflow stickiness we don't chase. Both are cloud-only with per-seat floors ($4K-$9K/yr minimums), are pivoting hard into hallucination-prone AI, and offer no portable/white-label/offline artifact. ChartHop also carries continuity risk from 2024-25 layoffs and scope sprawl. We selectively match ChartHop's org-chart credibility (span-of-control, future-dated forecast, restructure what-ifs) while framing ourselves as a complementary offline board-pack generator that consumes any HRIS export.

**Culture Amp (Threat 2 — adjacent/complementary).** Culture Amp is the engagement/sentiment leader — it owns the employee-voice "why" behind attrition via research-backed surveys, driver analysis, and a benchmark moat of 6,000+ companies, increasingly blending HRIS data into a unified People Analytics layer. It sits on the opposite half of people analytics from us (sentiment vs. operational/structural), is cloud-only, expensive ($50K-$200K+/yr enterprise), and survey-centric. Our play is to stay in the structural lane, add *offline* eNPS/engagement-CSV ingest plus a sentiment-vs-attrition cross-tab to capture ~80% of the story without becoming a cloud listening platform — and offer optional opt-in anonymized aggregate benchmark sharing to chip at the moat.

**orgvue & Anaplan Workforce Planning (Threat 2 — our clearest capability gap).** These tools model the *future* shape of an organization — harmonizing people/position/cost/skills data, then building side-by-side what-if scenarios (restructures, M&A, RIF, location moves) with live cost/headcount/capability impact. This forward-looking scenario engine, plus external labor-market/skills data (orgvue), is a capability we lack entirely, and it commands enterprise budgets (orgvue 3-yr TCO $150K-$600K+). The strategic recommendation: ship a *lightweight, offline, deterministic scenario sandbox* by treating a scenario as just another snapshot kind — clone the employee_master, edit roles, and see headcount + INR cost + span/layer deltas instantly in-tab. This is our single biggest gap to close and a strong differentiator when wrapped in privacy and portability.

**PwC Saratoga & Deloitte Human Capital (Threat 2).** Two Big-4 models: PwC's 40-year benchmarking franchise (30-40K+ validated benchmarks across 80+ peer groups) and Deloitte's consulting practice + accelerators + 2025 GenAI workforce suite. The benchmarking moat and board-level brand trust are real, but both require data to leave the building (Saratoga ingests raw HR/PII; Deloitte connects data ecosystems), are expensive with multi-year lock-in, and are slow ("week six" at best; annual study cadence for Saratoga). We counter with zero-egress privacy that turns the security review into a non-issue, instant single-file value, a deterministic auditable newsletter, and an optional manual overlay of *public* Saratoga Impact figures as a "no-benchmarking-by-design" complement.

**EY & KPMG (Threat 2).** Both sell advisory-led people analytics wrapped in HR transformation engagements — EY's experience index and EY.ai digital workers, KPMG's Powered Enterprise HR and AI-agent strategic workforce planning on Workday Adaptive. The value is the partner relationship, proprietary benchmarks, and change-management muscle, not a buy-and-run product. They are expensive, slow, alliance-locked (Workday/Microsoft/ServiceNow), and overkill for a recurring monthly report. Our answer is air-gapped self-serve value with no consultants, a deterministic auditable narrative, white-label resale for Indian consultancies, and a repeatable monthly cadence versus episodic projects.

**Predictive attrition / cost & scenario planning (Threat 2 — capability sweep).** Best-in-class predictive workforce analytics combines ML attrition scoring (random forest/GBT, survival models), SHAP/LIME explainability, and driver-based headcount/cost what-ifs — exemplified by Visier (needs 24-36 months of history) and the Anaplan/Planful/ChartHop planning camp. The decisive insight from the research: much of this is buildable *client-side and deterministically*. We can ship a transparent weighted/logistic-style attrition **risk index** (tenure band, time-since-promotion, comp-vs-band gap, overtime/leave, span, pay/performance changes) where every score breaks into named auditable contributors — that *is* our explainability — plus deterministic Kaplan-Meier time-to-attrition and pure-arithmetic INR cost planning, all offline with far less history than Visier requires.

**Frontier sweep — pay-equity / DEI / skills / ONA / GenAI / governance (Threat 2).** Across five adjacent frontiers, incumbents (Syndio/Trusaic/PayAnalytics, Gloat/Eightfold, Viva/Worklytics/Humanyze, Vee/Illuminate) are converging on cloud SaaS with AI on top — statistically defensible but privacy-exposed, fragmented point solutions with non-deterministic GenAI (cited 20-60% hallucination) and surveillance/works-council risk. The regulatory tailwind is significant: EU Pay Transparency Directive (transposition due **7 June 2026**, mandatory joint pay review on >5% unexplained gaps), India DPDP (penalties to ₹250 crore), and the EU AI Act classifying HR as high-risk. This is a rich white-space map for an offline, deterministic, governance-by-architecture tool — detailed below.

## 3. WHITE SPACE — Capabilities Incumbents Under-Serve

These are defensible openings where our architecture (offline, single-file, white-label, deterministic) is not merely cheaper but *structurally different* — things cloud/AI/enterprise incumbents cannot easily replicate.

1. **Radical privacy / zero data egress as the core product, not a tier.** Every competitor — without exception — requires PII to leave the customer's control (cloud ingestion, data submission, or alliance-platform connection). A single `dist/index.html` where PII never leaves the tab directly dissolves the months-long security review, India DPDP/data-localization blocker, works-council objection, and air-gap requirement. This is the one thing none of them can copy without abandoning their model.

2. **Deterministic, auditable narrative as a *feature*, not a gap.** The entire market is racing toward GenAI narratives (Vee, Illuminate, Joule, AI Coach, ChartHop AI) with acknowledged hallucination and explainability problems. A rule-based monthly CHRO newsletter where "every sentence traces to a number" — reproducible, defensible in a board meeting, requiring zero AI-governance approval — is a counter-positioned trust feature for compliance/legal/regulated/works-council buyers wary of LLMs.

3. **True zero-infrastructure, minutes-to-value.** Against 8–16 week (Visier), 4–18 month (Workday), ~100-day-to-year (SAP/Oracle), and "week six" (Deloitte) timelines, "open the file, load a CSV, get the CHRO newsletter today" — no servers, no integrations, no data-engineering project, no per-employee SaaS fee — is an unmatched time-to-value and TCO story.

4. **White-label / portable single-file distribution.** No incumbent offers a rebrandable, self-contained artifact a consultancy, PEO, fractional CHRO, or payroll/HRMS vendor can hand to many clients under their own brand. This is a distinct channel play — productizing what the Big 4 deliver as billable hours and what tenant-locked SaaS structurally cannot.

5. **A lightweight OFFLINE scenario sandbox (close the org-design gap).** The clearest *capability* gap is forward-looking what-if modeling (orgvue/Anaplan/ChartHop). By treating a scenario as just another snapshot kind, we can offer deterministic in-browser "plan vs. actual" — add/remove/move roles, see headcount + INR cost + span/layer deltas, diff scenarios side-by-side, email the what-if inside the gzipped workspace — with zero seats, zero cloud, zero per-employee fees.

6. **Deterministic, client-side predictive layer.** A transparent attrition **risk index** and Kaplan-Meier time-to-attrition built from snapshots we already store — explainable-by-construction, requiring far less history than Visier's 24-36 months. Optional opt-in in-browser ML (XGBoost-via-WASM / TF.js logistic regression, proven on small datasets) keeps the default deterministic while offering a "train on my own history, data never leaves the tab" path.

7. **Privacy-resilient pay-equity & governance modules.** A rule-based pay-gap module by gender/location/grade with a transparent methodology, a built-in "EU >5% unexplained-gap" flag, and a remediation-cost simulator — defensibility without a regression black box or cloud upload. Pair with an exportable **governance one-pager** (no network calls, no PII egress, deterministic, audit-log of inputs) mapping to DPDP / GDPR / EU AI Act high-risk concerns, turning compliance from a checkbox into the sales pitch.

8. **Survey-free, consent-safe "org/collaboration" and skills views.** ONA-flavored insight (reporting lines, span, cross-unit movement) from HRIS data already loaded — *without* mining employee communications — sidesteps the surveillance/works-council backlash incumbent ONA tools (Viva/Worklytics/Humanyze) carry. Add a privacy-resilient skills layer where users import their *own* taxonomy and compute coverage/gaps/adjacencies deterministically — skills intelligence with no opaque AI ontology or PII egress.

9. **Offline engagement ingest to neutralize the one real adjacency.** Culture Amp/Glint's clear edge is the employee-voice "why." A built-in eNPS/engagement-index CSV import (Google/Microsoft Forms, or even a Culture Amp export) plus a deterministic "sentiment vs. attrition" cross-tab captures ~80% of that story offline — closing the gap without becoming a cloud listening platform.

10. **India context as a deliberate down-market wedge.** INR formatting, Indian number grouping (lakh/crore), and India HR-ops framing are an explicit non-focus for every US/global-first incumbent — a localized moat for the SMB/mid-market and consultant channel they price out.

11. **2025-backlash-safe DEI posture.** Neutral, internal-only "representation & equity" analytics that run locally and are *never published* match the "strip the label, keep the work / quiet commitment" posture enterprises are adopting amid DEI political backlash — exactly the privacy-and-discretion profile cloud dashboards can't promise.

## 4. Where the Market Is Heading

- **GenAI narratives are becoming table-stakes — and a trust liability.** Visier (Vee), Workday (Illuminate storyteller), SAP (Joule), Culture Amp (AI Coach), and ChartHop (ChartHop AI) all now auto-generate prose/NL Q&A. But hallucination rates cited at 20-60%, the EU AI Act classifying HR as high-risk (Annex III), and EDPB scrutiny of incorrect personal-data outputs make *non-deterministic* narrative a growing compliance risk. **Implication:** our deterministic engine is increasingly valuable precisely *because* the market went the other way — lead with "auditable, reproducible, no AI-governance burden."

- **Agentic AI is the next frontier — moving from dashboards to action.** Workday Illuminate runs review cycles and workforce plans; Visier ships a Manager Agent; Anaplan has a "Workforce Analyst" agent; Deloitte/EY/KPMG push GenAI "digital workers." **Implication:** we will not (and need not) chase agentic action — but we should expect buyers to ask "does it do AI?" and have a crisp counter-narrative: deterministic, owner-tagged action plans that a human executes, with full auditability.

- **Skills intelligence is consolidating around AI ontologies.** Gloat and Eightfold build self-evolving skills knowledge graphs from billions of signals to power internal talent marketplaces and build/buy/borrow planning. **Implication:** the full ontology is an uncrossable moat (external data + AI), but a *bring-your-own-taxonomy* deterministic coverage/gap view is a credible, privacy-safe slice.

- **Pay equity & pay transparency are moving onto the budget by regulation.** The EU Pay Transparency Directive (transposition due **7 June 2026**, mandatory joint pay review on >5% unexplained gaps), US state pay laws, and India DPDP are forcing pay-equity tooling onto the roadmap (Syndio, Trusaic, PayAnalytics). **Implication:** a deterministic, offline pay-gap + remediation-simulator module with a built-in ">5% flag" is a timely, defensible feature — compliance-grade without the cloud.

- **ONA is rising but colliding with surveillance backlash.** Viva Insights, Worklytics, and Humanyze mine M365/Slack/Teams metadata for collaboration networks, but face works-council, ethics, and DPDP-consent resistance. **Implication:** an HRIS-derived, *survey-and-comms-free* org/collaboration view is the safe version of this trend.

- **Governance & data residency are becoming purchase gatekeepers, not afterthoughts.** SOC 2, GDPR, India DPDP (penalties to ₹250 crore), the EU AI Act, and NYC LL144 (whose enforcement a 2026 Comptroller audit found "ineffective") mean compliance posture increasingly *decides* deals. **Implication:** our strongest tailwind — "governance by architecture" (offline, deterministic, no PII egress, audit-logged inputs) — converts the industry's hardest emerging requirement into our default state. The market is moving toward AI, cloud, and consolidation; our durable bet is to move deliberately in the opposite direction on privacy, determinism, and portability for the buyers that consolidation leaves behind.
