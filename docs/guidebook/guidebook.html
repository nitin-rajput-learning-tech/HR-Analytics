1 · Executive overview

One place to see, diagnose and act on the people function

The HR Analytics platform turns the workbooks your teams already produce — the Keka employee master, recruitment trackers, payroll registers, the L&D log — into a single, board-ready view of the workforce. It then goes a step further than a dashboard: a built-in HR Brain reads every domain, flags the issues that matter, explains the likely cause, and proposes a remedy plan — all computed on your own device.

The problem it solves

People data lives in silos — an HRMS export here, a recruiter's spreadsheet there, payroll in a third system. Pulling it together for a board pack is a manual monthly scramble, and by the time it's assembled it answers "what happened" but rarely "what should we do about it." This platform closes both gaps.

See

A unified dashboard across People, Talent Acquisition, Performance, Payroll, L&D and HR Operations — headcount, attrition, diversity, cost, funnel, completion, compliance.

Diagnose

The HR Brain inspects all domains and surfaces known & emerging issues, each with evidence, a likely root cause and a step-by-step remedy — plus an HR Health score and trend.

Decide

A prioritised Now / Next / Later roadmap with impact, effort and the rupee value at stake — and a Scenario Planner to cost hiring and restructuring before you commit.

Report

A one-click monthly Newsletter — a polished, print-ready board pack with an executive brief, a scorecard, the diagnosis and a prioritised action plan.

What makes it different

  • It runs entirely on your machine. No server, no cloud, no AI service. Sensitive people data never leaves the device — a non-negotiable for HR.
  • It recommends, not just reports. The HR Brain is a deterministic, transparent rules engine: every finding shows the exact evidence and logic behind it. It is decision support you can audit, not a black box.
  • It works with the data you already have and degrades gracefully — start with the employee master alone and switch on each function as its feed comes online.
  • It is white-label and board-ready out of the box.
The honest framing
The HR Brain is a sophisticated set of HR heuristics, not an oracle. Benchmarks are illustrative reference bands until calibrated to your sector; cost-per-hire and severance figures are estimates. Every recommendation is a starting point for a human decision — and the tool always shows its working.
2 · How it works

Architecture, privacy & the data model

A single file that runs in your browser

The entire platform is one self-contained file (about 2.8 MB) that opens in any modern browser. There is nothing to install, no database to provision and no network connection required. You can run it from a shared drive, email it to a colleague, or host it behind your own SSO — it behaves identically.

ConcernHow the platform handles it
Data residencyAll processing is in-browser, on your device. No data is ever transmitted anywhere.
PersistenceYour working data is auto-saved locally in the browser. You can also export a single encrypted, compressed workspace file to move between machines.
SecurityOptional passphrase encryption on the saved workspace. No credentials, no accounts, no telemetry.
AI / external servicesNone. The HR Brain's narrative and recommendations are generated by local, deterministic rules.

The "true HR suite" data model

The platform spans the CHRO's direct functions. Each team submits a standardised workbook; the suite ingests each as its own dataset and computes that domain's metrics, then layers cross-functional intelligence on top.

The platform is built to consume both the system-generated employee master (Keka) and the snapshot your HR team maintains by hand — treating the HR-maintained file as the master where they differ, and surfacing any disagreement between the two so headcount is never quietly wrong.
3 · People Analytics

The workforce dashboard

The default view: a complete read of your active workforce, organised into tabs for Workforce, Diversity, Attrition & Risk, Movement & Org, and Pay & Cost. Every chart and table can be filtered (by department, location, level, etc.) and exported.

How to use it

1
Pick a lens. Use the sub-tabs (Workforce, Diversity, Attrition & Risk, Movement & Org, Pay & Cost) to move between the major people questions.
2
Filter. Narrow to a department, entity or location with the filter bar; every KPI and chart updates instantly. Save a filter combination as a named view to return to it.
3
Compare periods. When you have more than one monthly snapshot, KPI cards show the change versus the prior period, coloured for good/bad direction.
4
Export. Any table exports to CSV; any chart to PNG for slides.
4 · HR Brain — the flagship

An on-device recommendation engine

HR Brain reads every domain at once and produces a CHRO-grade diagnosis: an HR Health score with period-over-period direction, a ranked list of findings (each with evidence, a likely cause and a remedy plan), a prioritised action roadmap, and an HR maturity assessment. It is fully local and deterministic — no AI service is involved.

Reading the page, top to bottom

ElementWhat it tells you
HR Health score (0–100)An overall measure that falls as material issues accumulate (a critical issue costs far more than a minor one). The band reads Excellent → Critical.
Trend chip (e.g. ▼ −4 vs Apr 2026)Direction versus the prior period — the single most exec-relevant signal. Computed by re-running the diagnosis on last period's data.
Summary chipsCounts by severity and confidence, plus "N new this period" — how many issues newly emerged.
Finding cardsEach issue, ranked by severity, with: the evidence that triggered it, a likely reason, a remedy plan, the accountable owner, and a "View the evidence" deep-link to the underlying analytic.

Known vs. emerging, new vs. resolved

Each finding is tagged Known (a hard threshold breached), Likely or Possible (inferred or early-warning). Findings that appeared this period carry a blue NEW badge; issues that cleared since last period are celebrated in a "Resolved since last period" line — so the page shows progress, not just problems.

What the Brain diagnoses today
Retention (compounding risk, regrettable & early attrition, department hotspots), pay equity, statutory compliance, performance management (review completion, PIP load, rating calibration), talent-acquisition throughput (offer-accept, ageing requisitions), L&D coverage & mandatory-training compliance, employee engagement, organisation design, workforce-cost concentration, HR-operations risk (contract renewals, asset recovery), data-source reconciliation, plus benchmark and early-warning watch lists.
4 · HR Brain — continued

From diagnosis to a prioritised plan

Below the findings, HR Brain sequences the work into a consulting-style roadmap and scores your HR maturity.

The action roadmap

Every finding is placed on an impact × effort grid and sorted into three horizons. Quick wins and anything critical go Now; high-impact major initiatives go Next; low-impact or high-effort/low-payoff items go Later. The single highest-priority retention initiative carries an estimated rupee value at stake (annual attrition replacement cost) so the business case is explicit.

HR maturity assessment

Nine capability dimensions — Talent Acquisition, Performance, Reward & Pay Equity, Retention, Engagement, Diversity & Inclusion, Learning & Development, Org Design, and HR Operations & Compliance — are each scored 1–5 (Ad-hoc → Optimised) from your data, with a transparent basis and a "to advance" hint. Dimensions degrade when secondary signals stack (e.g. Performance drops when the PIP load is elevated on top of a completion gap).

How to work it in a monthly review
Start at the health trend (are we improving?), scan the NEW badges (what just emerged?), then work the Now column of the roadmap with the named owners. Re-check next month — the trend and new/resolved markers tell you whether the actions are landing.

6 · Scorecard

Targets & industry benchmarks

The management-by-objective layer: your headline KPIs as a RAG (red/amber/green) scorecard against targets you set, with period-over-period movement and an industry benchmark band for context.

How to use it

1
Set targets. Edit any KPI's target inline; the RAG status recomputes immediately. Targets persist with your workspace.
2
Calibrate benchmarks. The platform ships illustrative industry bands; click "Edit benchmarks" to replace them with sourced figures for your sector and region.
3
Read the position. Each KPI shows whether you're ▲ better, ● typical, or ▼ worse than the band — direction-aware, so "lower is better" metrics read correctly.
Honesty by design
The default benchmark bands are general reference ranges, not a sourced survey — the platform labels them as such, and the Brain treats "below benchmark" as a likely, not confirmed, signal until you supply real peer data.
7 · Scenario Planner

Model hires, cuts & restructures — with the full cost

A what-if sandbox for proposals. Build a plan from hire / cut / move operations and watch the headcount and cost impact recompute instantly — including the upfront one-time costs a board paper needs.

What it computes

OutputMeaning
Active headcountResulting headcount and the change vs baseline, by department.
Monthly & annualised costThe ongoing run-rate change, using real per-department payroll cost (or an assumption you set).
One-time hiring costUpfront recruitment spend = external hires × cost-per-hire.
One-time exit costSeverance = months of pay × the cost of the roles cut (an editable assumption).
Year-1 cash impactThe headline figure: run-rate ×12 plus the one-time costs — for a restructuring it nets the saving against severance, showing the payback.

Export the whole scenario — operations, summary and per-department impact — to CSV to attach to a proposal.

8 · HR Newsletter

The automated board pack

One click assembles a polished, print-ready monthly newsletter for the CHRO and function heads — the deliverable a consulting firm would charge for, generated from your live data in seconds.

What's in the pack

  • CHRO executive brief — a one-paragraph summary, headline KPIs, wins, risks and movers, led by a "Since <last period>: HR Health ▼ −4 · N new · M resolved" digest.
  • Scorecard vs targets — with the industry-benchmark column.
  • HR Brain diagnosis — health & trend, findings (each with reason and remedy), the Now/Next/Later roadmap with value at stake, and the maturity assessment.
  • One section per function — and a prioritised, owner-tagged action plan to close.

How to use it

1
Open the Newsletter tab — it builds automatically from whatever data is loaded.
2
Print to PDF — the layout is print-optimised (one function per page, tables and cards never split). Use the browser's "Save as PDF".
3
Or export the facts pack — a plain-Markdown version to paste into email or Slack.
9 · Data Intake & Directory

Getting your data in

Data Intake is where each team loads its workbook. The platform provides a standard template per domain, reads the as-of date automatically, and validates as it goes.

How to use it

1
Pick the domain (Employee Master, Talent Acquisition, Performance, Payroll, L&D, HR Operations, Engagement).
2
Download the template if the team needs the exact columns — it lists required vs optional fields and the join key.
3
Upload the file. The as-of date is read from the filename or the export footer; override it only if needed. The platform confirms what it loaded and flags any issues.

The Directory

A searchable, filterable, sortable roster of the full employee population, with saved views and CSV export — the operational lookup that complements the analytical views.

Graceful by design
It works on day one with just the employee master, accepts both per-employee detail and aggregate-only variants, and ignores fields you've retired (for example, the legacy pay-band column).
10 · Implementation plan

A phased rollout

You can be live with real analytics in an afternoon, then switch on each function as its feed becomes available. A suggested sequence:

Phase 0  Stand up — Week 1

  • Open the platform and upload the current employee master (the Keka export) plus the HR-maintained snapshot. People Analytics, the Directory and the first HR Brain findings light up immediately.
  • Apply your branding (logo, colours, name) on the Branding tab.
  • Confirm the two feeds reconcile; resolve any headcount disagreement.

Phase 1  Bring the functions online — Weeks 2–6

Onboard one feed at a time, in priority order. Each unlocks its Function Analytics tab and the matching HR Brain rules.

WeekFeedUnlocks
2Talent AcquisitionFunnel, offer-accept, ageing requisitions, cost-of-hire
3Performance (PMS)Completion, 9-box, PIP load, calibration
4Payroll + statutoryCost per head, statutory compliance, scenario cost basis
5Learning & DevelopmentCoverage, mandatory-training compliance, spend
6HR Operations + EngagementContracts, assets, lifecycle; eNPS

Phase 2  Calibrate — Week 7

  • Set targets on the Scorecard to your commitments.
  • Replace the illustrative benchmark bands with sourced figures for your sector and region.
  • Set the Scenario cost assumptions (cost-per-hire, severance months) for your context.

Phase 3  Operate — ongoing monthly cadence

1
Refresh feeds after month-end close (each owner re-uploads their workbook).
2
Review HR Brain — health trend, new issues, the Now column of the roadmap.
3
Generate the Newsletter and circulate the board pack.
4
Action & track — assign the roadmap items to owners; next month's trend and resolved markers confirm whether they landed.
